\begin{abstract}
This manuscript presents the Back-End Law, a universal structural mechanism governing collapse, identity formation, irreversibility, and the emergence of stable reality across substrates. The framework integrates collapse dynamics, the Substrate Operator, the Gate, the Irreversibility Theorem, the Universal Compiler, and the AST-450 audit archive. \[

\[

\[
R: s^{-} \mapsto s^{+} \quad \text{(non-unitary, irreversible)}

Constraint Function:
\[
\Phi: S \rightarrow \{0,1\}
\]

Permitted Set:
\[
P = \{ s \mid \Phi(s) = 1 \}
\]

Forbidden Set:
\[
F = \{ s \mid \Phi(s) = 0 \}
\]

Identity Invariant:
\[
I(s^{-}) = I(s^{+})
\]

Gate Definition:
\[
G = \{ s \mid \chi(s) - C(s) > \theta \ \wedge \ \Phi(s) = 1 \}
\]

Evolution Operator:
\[
T(s) = R(s) \cap P
\]

Irreversibility Theorem:
\[
s \rightarrow s' \text{ through } G \quad \Rightarrow \quad \nexists \{s_t\}: s_0 = s', \ s_n = s, \ s_{t+1} \in T(s_t)
\]

\subsubsection*{4. Symmetry Breaking $\Sigma$}

Turbulence Term:
\[
\eta(s) = \text{irreducible asymmetry}
\]

Stabilization Region:
\[
A = \{ s \mid \chi(s) - C(s) \le \theta \}
\]
